GUIDE FOR BETTER LIVING

AFTER KNEE REPLACEMENT

FOREWORD

Knee replacement surgery is being increasingly accepted by patients with severe osteoarthritis for relief from pain and disability, thereby improving overall quality of life. Now even younger individuals are undergoing knee replacement. They not only expect relief from pain, but also want to be able to participate in sports and other recreational activities. Advances in technology, surgical methods, and effective physical rehabilitation have resulted in predictable and better outcomes. Individuals become so comfortable that they forget about their artificial knee.

While acceptance for knee replacement is constantly on the rise, there are gaps in knowledge and apprehensions about surgery and rehabilitation. Patients and family members want to know about post-operative pain, resumption of activities of daily living, going back to work, driving, sports, etc. This guide is a beneficial resource for educating patients about the chain of events before, during and after surgery. This also provides a comprehensive information about do’s and don’ts to make them confident and assured when planning for knee replacement.

This guide is developed by experienced healthcare providers from two premiere institutions with several decades of experience in the field of knee arthroplasty. It is simple, easy to understand and follow, and above all meets the much-needed demand for such a resource.

We are hopeful that this guide will be extremely useful for all those undergoing knee replacement.
